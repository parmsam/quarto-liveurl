The Problem: Sequential Bottlenecks

Before Transformers, sequence modelling relied on RNNs and LSTMs.

Key limitations:

  • Hidden state \(h_t\) depends on \(h_{t-1}\) → computation is inherently sequential
  • Long sequences → vanishing gradients, forgetting early context
  • Hard to parallelise → slow to train on modern hardware

“The dominant sequence transduction models are based on complex recurrent or convolutional neural networks.” — §1

Transformers eliminate recurrence entirely. Every position attends to every other position in parallel.

The Core Idea: Self-Attention

Instead of passing state through time, each token directly attends to all other tokens in the sequence.

Three learned projections per token:

Matrix Role
Q (Query) What am I looking for?
K (Key) What do I offer?
V (Value) What do I contribute if selected?

Scaled dot-product attention:

\[\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V\]

The \(\sqrt{d_k}\) scaling prevents the dot products from growing too large and pushing softmax into regions of tiny gradients.

Multi-Head Attention

Running attention once captures one type of relationship. Multi-head attention runs it \(h\) times in parallel with different learned projections.

\[\text{MultiHead}(Q,K,V) = \text{Concat}(\text{head}_1, \ldots, \text{head}_h)\,W^O\]

\[\text{head}_i = \text{Attention}(QW^Q_i,\, KW^K_i,\, VW^V_i)\]

The paper uses \(h = 8\) heads with \(d_k = d_v = 64\).

Each head can learn a different aspect of context — syntax, coreference, long-range dependencies — simultaneously.

The Transformer Architecture

  • Figure 1 — Full Architecture

The model follows an encoder–decoder design, used here for translation.

Component Layers
Encoder 6 identical layers
Decoder 6 identical layers

Each layer has two sub-layers:

  1. Multi-head self-attention
  2. Position-wise feed-forward network (two linear transforms, ReLU in between)

Every sub-layer wraps a residual connection followed by layer normalisation: \[\text{LayerNorm}(x + \text{Sublayer}(x))\]

Encoder in Detail

The encoder maps an input sequence \((x_1, \ldots, x_n)\) to continuous representations \(\mathbf{z} = (z_1, \ldots, z_n)\).

Each of the 6 encoder layers:

Input embeddings + positional encoding
        ↓
[Multi-Head Self-Attention] → Add & Norm
        ↓
[Feed-Forward Network]      → Add & Norm
        ↓
Output to next layer (or decoder)

Self-attention here is bidirectional — every position can attend to every other position in both directions.

Decoder in Detail

The decoder generates the output sequence one token at a time.

Each of the 6 decoder layers has three sub-layers:

Output embeddings + positional encoding
        ↓
[Masked Multi-Head Self-Attention] → Add & Norm   ← prevents attending to future
        ↓
[Cross-Attention over encoder output] → Add & Norm ← connects encoder to decoder
        ↓
[Feed-Forward Network]               → Add & Norm

Masking ensures position \(i\) can only attend to positions \(< i\), preserving the auto-regressive property during training.

Positional Encoding

Attention is permutation-invariant — it has no built-in sense of order. Positional encodings inject sequence position information.

The paper uses fixed sine and cosine functions:

\[PE_{(pos,\, 2i)} = \sin\!\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{2i/d_{\text{model}}}}\right)\] \[PE_{(pos,\, 2i+1)} = \cos\!\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{2i/d_{\text{model}}}}\right)\]

Added directly to the token embeddings before the first layer.

Why sinusoids? Each frequency allows the model to easily learn to attend by relative position, and the encoding generalises to sequence lengths unseen during training.

Training & Results

  • §5 Training
  • §6 Results

Training setup:

  • Adam optimiser with warm-up learning rate schedule
  • Dropout (rate 0.1) on sub-layer outputs and embeddings
  • Label smoothing (\(\epsilon_{ls} = 0.1\))
  • Trained on 8 × P100 GPUs

Translation benchmarks (WMT 2014):

Task BLEU Previous SOTA
English → German 28.4 26.4
English → French 41.0 40.5

Trained in 12 hours vs. days for prior ensemble models.

Why It Mattered

The Transformer became the backbone of essentially every major language model that followed.

Direct descendants:

Model Year Built on
BERT 2018 Encoder only
GPT series 2018–present Decoder only
T5 2019 Encoder–decoder
Vision Transformer (ViT) 2020 Encoder, image patches
LLaMA, Mistral, Claude… 2023–present Decoder only

The self-attention mechanism is now the default building block for sequence modelling across language, vision, audio, and biology.

The paper has accumulated over 100,000 citations as of 2024.
